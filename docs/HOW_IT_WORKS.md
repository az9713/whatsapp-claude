### Why Do You Need to Scan?

WhatsApp requires this for security. It proves:
1. **You own the phone number** - Only you can scan from your phone
2. **You authorize this device** - You're explicitly allowing the bot to receive messages
3. **End-to-end encryption** - Messages are still encrypted; the bot just has permission to read yours

### After Scanning

Once scanned, your phone and the bot are "linked devices." WhatsApp will:
- Send a copy of every message to both your phone AND the bot
- The bot only processes messages **from you, to yourself**
- Other people's messages are ignored

### What If Someone Else Messages You?

```
Their message: "Hey, can you help me?"
Bot sees:      fromMe: false
Bot action:    IGNORED (not processed)

Your message:  "/claude hello"
Bot sees:      fromMe: true, starts with "/claude "
Bot action:    PROCESSED
```

## Summary

The "magic" is actually a chain of well-established technologies:

1. **WhatsApp** delivers your message to an invisible Chrome browser on your laptop
2. **Node.js** receives the message and extracts your command
3. **Claude Code** understands your intent and decides what to do
4. **Various tools** (Remotion, TTS, etc.) execute the actual task
5. **The response** travels back through WhatsApp to your iPhone

All running on **your laptop**, controlled by **your WhatsApp**, executing **your commands**.

That's not magic - it's just good engineering! But it sure feels like magic when you're lying on the couch and your computer creates a video for you.
